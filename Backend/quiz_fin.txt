- Questions:
    1. What is the difference between stocks and bonds in finance?
    2. How does compound interest work in financial investments?
    3. What is the concept of diversification in investment portfolios?
    4. Explain the difference between a traditional IRA and a Roth IRA.
    5. How do financial ratios like P/E ratio and debt-to-equity ratio help in analyzing a company's financial health?

- Answers:
    1. Stocks represent ownership in a company, while bonds represent debt owed by a company.
    2. Compound interest is the interest calculated on the initial principal and also on the accumulated interest of previous periods.
    3. Diversification is the strategy of spreading investments across different assets to reduce risk.
    4. A traditional IRA allows for tax-deferred contributions and withdrawals are taxed, while a Roth IRA allows for tax-free withdrawals but contributions are not tax-deductible.
    5. P/E ratio (Price-to-Earnings ratio) helps in evaluating a company's stock price relative to its earnings, while debt-to-equity ratio measures a company's financial leverage by comparing its debt to its equity.
